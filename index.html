Thesis work at Fatshark

My Master thesis titled "Real-time Raytracing and Screen-space Ambient Occlusion" explores how emerging technology like DirectX Raytracing (DXR) fit with other state-of-the art approaches that use screen-space data for ambient occlusion. In total 7 methods including my proposed variant were studied in depth and deployed on the Unity engine. I produced reference raytraced scenes using Maya, and used Structural Similarity (SSIM) score as an objective metric for comparing the output quality of different solutions.

King of the Cube

An augmented reality multiplayer game developed as part of the Advanced Graphics and Interaction course at KTH, King of the Cube incorporates elements from famous 2D-side-scrollers like Super Mario and “King of the Hill” games. Since its start in August 2018, the project has been awarded two CAwards and will be displayed as an exhibit at Tekniska Museet's Play Beyond Play.

Oink!

For this project, I wanted to experiment with hyper-casual mobile games, and also use the medium to make the topic of the meat industry and its effect on the environment more accessible. The aim of Oink! is to save animals from a slaughterhouse. The player breaks into a slaughterhouse at night to release the animals. By building and connecting conveyor belts to the exit, the livestock can be transported to safety. Oink! is inspired by “Angry Birds” and “World of Goo”, and uses a unique game mechanic for building the conveyor belts.

Parallelized GPU Raytracing with Acceleration Structures

As part of an open-ended final project for Computer Graphics course at KTH, I worked alongside my colleague, Forrest, to create a GPU raytracer. It uses KD-trees and a GPU implementation using OpenCL 2.0. We demonstrated a 99.9 % drop in render time for larger models compared to naïve CPU-based Raytracer.

Embodied Social Agents Lab

Over the summer I investigated the process of generating a photorealistic social robot in a Microsoft Hololens environment to replicate interaction with the physical reference Pepper robot. I looked into the effects of dynamic lighting, reflection probes , and post-processing effects (e.g. FXAA, SSAO, Bloom, etc.) on render quality.

Neuron Tracer

For the Advanced Topics in Computer Graphics, I chose to reproduce the solution presented in the paper "A Virtual Reality Visualization Tool for Neuron Tracing" . This project was a great opportunity for me to experiment with the potential of VR as a scientific tool. I compared different approaches such as raymarching and isosurfaces to produce a responsive virtual reality environment based on raw medical images of neurons.
